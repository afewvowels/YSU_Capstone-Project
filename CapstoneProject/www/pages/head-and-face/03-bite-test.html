Head & Face

Bite Test

Patient Position

Sitting or standing.

Examiner Position

in front of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

  1. The examiner holds a tongue depresser in one hand, and places the distal end of the depressor in the patient's mouth.
  2. The patient is then instructed to bite down as hard as he/she can.
  3. The examiner attempts to pull the tongue depresser out of the patient's mouth.

Positive Test

Patient will not be able to keep their bite on the tongue depressor.

Implications

Cranial Nerve V (Trigeminal nerve) pathology.
