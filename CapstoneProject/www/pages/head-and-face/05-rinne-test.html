Head & Face

Rinne Test

Patient Position

Sitting upright in chair.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the ear tested.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner will use a 512 Hz tuning fork.
  2. The examiner will then either flick the tuning fork or tap it against themself or against an object, causing it to vibrate.
  3. The examiner will then place the tuning fork, with the handle side, on the mastoid process behind the ear, and have it there until the patient says they cannot hear it anymore.
  4. Then the examiner places the opposite end of the tuning fork near the patient's external auditory canal.
  5. The examiner then asks the patient to tell him/her when they do not hear it anymore.
  6. This test is done bilaterally.

Positive Test

Patient will not hear the tuning fork once the examiner moves it from the mastoid process.

Implications

Problem with conduction into the ear; hearing loss.
