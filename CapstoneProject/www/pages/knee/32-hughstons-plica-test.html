Knee

Hughston's Plica Test

Patient Position

Lying supine with involved knee extended and relaxed

Examiner Position

Examiner stands on the involved side

Examiner Hand Position

Places the heel of one hand over the lateral border of the patella, with the fingers of the same hand positioned over the medial femoral condyle, while other hand is cupping the subject's heel

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively flexes and extends the subject's knee while simultaneously internally rotating the tibia and pushing the patella medially

Positive Test

Pain and/or popping over the medial aspect of the knee

Implications

Abnormal plica

Modifications/Comments

This test may lead to further irritation of the structure
