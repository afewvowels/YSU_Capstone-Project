Knee

Hughston's Posterolateral Drawer

Patient Position

lying supine with hip flexed 45 degrees and knee at 90 degrees, tibia externally rotated 20-30 degrees.

Examiner Position

lying supine with hip flexed 45 degrees and knee at 90 degrees, tibia internally rotated 20-30 degrees.

Examiner Hand Position

Both hands are placed behind the subject's proximal tibia and thumbs on the tibial plateau.

Evaluation Procedure

Apply a posterior force to the proximal tibia.

Positive Test

Increased posterior tibial displacement compared bilaterally.

Implications

Posterolateral rotary instability.
