Knee

Quad Active Test

Patient Position

Lying supine with knee flexed to 90 degrees

Examiner Position

At the side of the patient

Examiner Hand Position

One hand stabilizes the distal tibia, and the other hand stabilizes the distal femur

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient is asked to slide the foot forward by contracting the quadriceps while the examiner applies resistance at the tibia.

Positive Test

Anterior translation of the tibia

Implications

Grade II or III posterior cruciate ligament sprain

Modifications/Comments

test is more accurate with higher grade lesions

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 54%
  • Specificty: 97%

Starkey C, Brown SD. Examination of Orthopedic & Athletic Injuries. 4th ed. Philadelphia: F.A. Davis Company; 2015.
