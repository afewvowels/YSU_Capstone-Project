Foot & Toe

Navicular Drop Test

Back

Patient Position

Seated with both feet on floor.

Examiner Position

Kneeling in front of patient.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

  1. With the patient seated, a dot is placed on the navicular tuberosity.
  2. While the seated patient's foot is still planted on ground, an index card is placed next to the medial longitudinal arch.
  3. Patient now stands with body weight distributed evenly on both feet relaxed.
  4. Palpate the navicular tuberosity while patient is standing, and note it's level on the index card.
  5. Note the relative displacement of the navicular from seated to standing by measuring the marks in millimeters.

Positive Test

Navicular Drop between seated and standing position.

Implications

  • >2.3 cm = very flexible
  • 1.8-2.3 cm = flexibile
  • 0.6-1.8 cm = normal
  • 0.0-0.6 cm = rigid

Modifications/Comments

Measure of navicular drop is relative to pronation during gait.
