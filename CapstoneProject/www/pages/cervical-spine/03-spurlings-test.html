Cervical Spine

Spurling's Test

Patient Position

Seated.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Both hands are interlocked on top of the patient's head.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient extends and laterally flexes their head.
  2. Examiner then applies an axial load on top of the patient's head.
  3. Test is done bilaterally.

Positive Test

Pain radiating down the patient's arm.

Implications

Nerve root impingement by narrowing of the neural foramina.

Modifications/Comments

Rule out a cervical fracture or instability before performing test.

Evidence

N/A
