Cervical Spine

Cervical Distraction Test

Patient Position

Lying supine on table.

Examiner Position

At the head of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Examiner's hands on each side of the patient's mandible, around the mandibular angle.

Evaluation Procedure

Examiner applies distraction to the patient's head

Positive Test

Patient's symptoms are relieved or reduced.

Implications

Compression of the facet joints and/or stenosis of the neural foramina.

Modifications/Comments

Can also apply traction with examiner's proximal hand around the patient's occiput and the distal hand on the patient's forearm.

Evidence

None

Evidence Resource

Starkey C, Brown SD. Examination of Orthopedic & Athletic Injuries. 4th ed. Philadelphia: F.A. Davis Company; 2015.
