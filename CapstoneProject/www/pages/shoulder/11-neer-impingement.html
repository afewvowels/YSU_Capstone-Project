Shoulder

Neer Impingement Test

Back

Patient Position

Sitting or standing with shoulder, elbow, and wrist in anatomical position.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand stabilizes the scapula, the other hand grabs the patient's arm at wrist joint.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. With the elbow extended, the humerus is placed in internal rotation and the forearm is pronated.
  2. Move the shoulder through forward flexion.

Positive Test

Pain in the anterior or lateral shoulder (90 degrees-full flexion).

Implications

Impingement of the rotator cuff muscles (especially supraspinatus) or the long head of biceps brachii. Test may also present as a SLAP lesion.

Modifications/Comments

The accuracy of this test is challanged by the various possible outcomes that it produces.

Evidence

Impingement:

  • Sensitivity: 77%
  • Specificity: 53%

SLAP Lesion:

  • Sensitivity: 48%
  • Specificity: 40%

Evidence Resource
