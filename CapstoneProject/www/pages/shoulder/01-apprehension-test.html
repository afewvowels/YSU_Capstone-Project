Shoulder

Apprehension Test

Patient Position

Laying supine on table with shoulder abducted to 90 degrees and elbow flexed to 90 degrees.

Examiner Position

Beside patient on the involved side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand supporting humerus at midshaft and the other grasping the forearm proximal to the wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

Examiner passively externally rotates the GH joint by slowly applying pressure to the anterior forearm.

Positive Test

Patient displays apprehension that the shoulder may dislocate and resists further movement. Pain is presented in the anterior capsule.

Implications

Anterior capsule, inferiro GH ligament, or labrum have been damaged. Pain may be indicative of rotator cuff pathology. Deep posterior shoulder pain may be indicitive of internal impingement.

Modifications/Comments

Test should NOT be performed if there is obvious dislocation or subluxation. Follow up test with the relocation test.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 58%
  • Specificity: 78%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
