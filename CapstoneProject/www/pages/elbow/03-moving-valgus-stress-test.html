Elbow

Moving Valgus Stress Test

Patient Position

Seated or standing.

Examiner Position

Standing posterior to the patient's affected elbow.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the lateral epicondyle and the distal hand grasping the patient's distal forearm.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively abducts the patient's shoulder to 90 degrees.
  2. With the patient's elbow in full extension, the examiner then applies a valgus force to the elbow and then passively flexes the patient's affected elbow through the full range of motion.

Positive Test

Increased laxity, pain, or both when compared bilaterally.

Implications

Ulnar Collateral Ligament Sprain (anterior oblique or posterior oblique position).
