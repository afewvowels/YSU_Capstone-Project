Elbow

Posterolateral Rotary Instability Test (Pivot Shift)

Patient Position

Supine on the table.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand grasping the proximal forearm and the distal hand grasping the distal forearm near the wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. The examiner passively flexes the shoulder and elbow to 90 degrees, and passively supinates the forearm until the end range of motion.
  2. The examiner then places a valgus force to the elbow with an axial load, while fully extending the elbow.
  3. Then the examiner brings back the patient's elbow into flexion.

Positive Test

The patient's elbow subluxes as it is extended and relocates as it is flexed back into starting position.

Implications

Chronic instability of the elbow.
