Wrist & Hand

Phalen's Test

Patient Position

Standing.

Examiner Position

Standing in front of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

(See modifications).

Evaluation Procedure

Patient maximally flexes wrists by pushing the dorsal side of the hands together and holds for 1 minutes (see modifications).

Positive Test

Tingling develops or increases throughout the test.

Implications

Median nerve compression.

Modifications/Comments

Test may be performed unilaterally with the assistance of the clinician. The clinician supports the arm at the elbow and uses the other hand to apply maximal flexion of the wrist. Hold for 1 minute.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 66%
  • Specificity: 72%
