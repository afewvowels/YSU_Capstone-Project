Hip & Pelvis

Trendelenberg Test

Patient Position

Standing with weight evenly distributed on both feet.

Examiner Position

Standing behind patient, making sure PSIS and Iliac Crest are visible.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

Patient lifts leg opposite of side being tested.

Positive Test

Pelvis lowers on non-weight bearing side.

Implications

Glute Medius weakness.

Modifications/Comments

Nerve pathology may result in positive findings.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 64%
  • Specificity: 50%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
