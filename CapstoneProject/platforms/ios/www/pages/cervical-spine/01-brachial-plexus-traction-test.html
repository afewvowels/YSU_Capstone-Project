Cervical Spine

Brachial Plexus Traction Test

Back

Patient Position

Seated or Standing.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand is placed on side of patient's head. Distal hand is placed on the ipsilateral acromioclavicular joint.

Evaluation Procedure

Patient's head is laterally bent to the opposite side of the distal hand & the distal hand compresses the shoulder

Positive Test

Pain radiating through the upper arm.

Implications

  • Pain radiating on the opposite side of lateral flexion: Stretching of the brachial plexus.
  • Pain radiating on the same side of lateral flexion: Compression of the cervical nerve roots between two vertebrae.

Modifications/Comments

This test should not be done unless a cervical fracture or dislocation has been ruled out.
