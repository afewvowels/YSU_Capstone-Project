Angle of Torsion/Craig's Test

Back

Patient Position

Prone with involved knee flexed to 90 degrees.

Examiner Position

At side of patient.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand palpates the greater trochanter of the femur and the other hand grasps distal tibia.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner applies pressure downward along the shaft of the femur to compress the joint surfaces.
  2. Femur is internally and externally rotated with hip in multiple angles of flexion.

Positive Test

Angle is less than 15 degrees or greater than 20 degrees.

Implications

Modifications/Comments

If possible, can use a second examiner to measure with a goniometer. Compare bilaterally. Anteversion leads to toeing in/squinting patella and retroversion leads to toeing out/frog-eyed patella.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 89%
  • Specificity: 92%
