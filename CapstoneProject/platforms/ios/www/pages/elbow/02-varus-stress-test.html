Elbow

Varus Stress Test

Back

Patient Position

Seated or standing.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient's affected elbow.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the medial epicondyle and the distal hand grasping the patient's distal forearm.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively flexes the patient's elbow to 25 degrees.
  2. The examiner then applies a varus force to the elbow.
  3. The procedure is then done in multiple angles with the elbow flexed.

Positive Test

Increased laxity, pain, or both when compared bilaterally.

Implications

Radial Collateral Ligament Sprain.

Modifications/Comments

Gross laxity may also indicate damage to the annular ligament or accessory lateral collateral ligament. Laxity may also incicate a epiphyseal injury.
