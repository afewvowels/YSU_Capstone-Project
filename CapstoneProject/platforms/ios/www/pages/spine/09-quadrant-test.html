Thoracic & Lumbar Spine

Quadrant Test

Patient Position

Standing with feet shoulder width apart.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Grasping each of the patient's shoulders.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient extends the spine as far as possible.
  2. Next, the patient will sidebend and rotate to the affected side.
  3. The examiner will then provide overpressure through the shoulders.

Positive Test

Reproduction of patient's symptoms.

Implications

Compression of the lumbar nerve roots (radicular pain). Local pain indicates facet joint pathology, symptoms isolated to the PSIS indicate SI joint dysfunction.
