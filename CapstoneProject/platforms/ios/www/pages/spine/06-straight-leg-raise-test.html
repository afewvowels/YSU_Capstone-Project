Thoracic & Lumbar Spine

Straight Leg Raise Test/Lasegue

Back

Patient Position

supine.

Examiner Position

at the side of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

  1. The patient lies supine and actively raises the leg by flexing the hip to 90 degrees or until pain is elicited while keeping the knee extended.
  2. The patient is then instructed 10 degrees and the patient actively dorsiflexes the foot.

Positive Test

Pain, numbness, or tingling radiating in low back, glut, or down the leg.

Implications

lumbar/sacral pathology and/or disc herniation.

N/A
