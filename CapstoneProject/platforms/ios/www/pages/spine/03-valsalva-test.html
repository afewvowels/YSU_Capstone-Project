Thoracic & Lumbar Spine

Valsalva Test

Back

Patient Position

sitting at the edge of the table.

Examiner Position

at the side of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A

Evaluation Procedure

  1. the patient sits on the edge of the table and tightly clasps their hands together in front of their chest while they increase intrathecal pressure by contracting their abs and lower pelvic floor (such as a bowl movement) for approximately 10 seconds or until pain is elicited.
  2. The examiner observes the patient's pain level in the lumbar/thoracic region of the spine.

Positive Test

pain, numbness, or tingling into the extremities and/or at the site of vertebral disc pathology.

Implications

Vertebral disc pathology.

Modifications/Comments

Risks associated with this test include hypertension, stroke, and heart arrythmias.
