Hip & Pelvis

Ober's Test

Back

Patient Position

Lying on uninvolved side, knee is flexed to 90 degrees and hip is flexed to 45 degrees.

Examiner Position

Standing behind patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand stabilizes pelvis and distal hand supports the leg being tested along the medial aspect of the distal tibia.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively abducts and extends patient's hip.
  2. Hip then is allowed to passively adduct to the table.

Positive Test

Patient's involved knee does not adduct past horizontal.

Implications

IT band tightness.

Modifications/Comments

Test may be performed with involved leg straight.
