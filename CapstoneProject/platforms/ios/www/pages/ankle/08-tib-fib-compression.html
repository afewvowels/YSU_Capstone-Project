Ankle

Tib-Fib Compression (Squeeze)

Patient Position

Lying with the knee extended.

Examiner Position

Standing next to or infront of the injured leg.

Examiner Hand Position

Hands cupped behind tibia and fibula away from the site of pain<./p>

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Gentle compress the fibula and tibia, grandually applying more pressure if no symptoms of pain are elicitied.
  2. Progress towards site of pain until pain is elicited.

Positive Test

Pain is elicited, especially when it is away from the compressed area.

Implications

Gross fracture/stress fracture of the fibula. Syndesmosis sprain when pain is described at the distal tib-fib.

Modifications/Comments

Avoid applying too much pressure too early in the test. May experience false positive tib-fib syndesmosis sprain.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 30%
  • Specificity: 94%
