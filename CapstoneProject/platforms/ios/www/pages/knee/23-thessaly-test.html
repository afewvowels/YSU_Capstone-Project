Knee

Thessaly Test

Back

Patient Position

Standing on one leg with knee slightly flexed (5 degrees).

Examiner Position

Standing infront of patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Examiner holds both of subject's hands to provide support and balance.

Evaluation Procedure

Subject twists back and forth 3 times, rotating the knees and body internally and externally.

Positive Test

Medial/lateral joint line pain, clicking or locking of the knee.

Implications

Mensicus tear.

Modifications/Comments

Once the test is done in 5 degrees of flexion, the test is repeated with 20 degrees of flexion. Test should be done with uninvolved leg first.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: (5 degrees) 65% (20 degrees) 80%
  • Specificity: (5 degrees) 83% (20 degrees) 41%
