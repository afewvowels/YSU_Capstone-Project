Knee

Ballotable Patella/Patella Tap

Back

Patient Position

Lying supine with knee extened and quadriceps relaxed.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient of the side being tested.

Examiner Hand Position

Thumbs are placed on the lateral aspect of the patella and the index fingers are placed on the medial aspect of the patella, with the crease between the thumb and index finger of each hand on the superior and inferior poles of the patella respectively.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Superior hand pushes any fluid in the superior portion of the knee inferiorly toward the patella, opposite hand pushes any fluid in the inferior portion of the knee superiorly toward the patella
  2. a finger is used to press the patella down towards the patellar groove.

Positive Test

Patella depresses and strikes the patellar groove, and returns back to its former position.

Implications

Effusion within the joint capsule.

Modifications/Comments

Any rapid onset of effusion typically results from traumatic force, fracture, patellar dislocation, or ligament sprain.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 50%
  • Specificity: 54%
