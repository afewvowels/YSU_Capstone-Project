Foot & Toe

Windlass Test

Patient Position

Sitting on the edge of the examination table.

Examiner Position

At patient's foot.

Examiner Hand Position

No stabilizing hands needed.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient begins as non-weight bearing.
  2. Examines the postion of the medial longitudinal arch.
  3. Examiner instructs patient to stand so that the body weight is evenly distributed.
  4. Patient then performs a single leg heel raise on the limb being tested.
  5. Examiner notes position of medial longitudinal arch while patient is weight bearing in the single leg heel raise.

Positive Test

Pain is reproduced during single leg heel raise.

Implications

If the medial longitudinal arch disappears when weight bearing, pes planus is present. If no arch is present while non-weight bearing, a rigid pes planus is present.

Modifications/Comments

Test is meaningful when the medial longitudinal arch is present when the patient is non-weight bearing.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 24%
  • Specificity: 100%

Evidence Resource
