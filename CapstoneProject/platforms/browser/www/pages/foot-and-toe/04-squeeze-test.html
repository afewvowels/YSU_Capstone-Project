Foot & Toe

Long Bone Compression Test/Compression (Squeeze Test)

Back

Patient Position

Lying with the knee extended.

Examiner Position

Standing next to or in front of the injured leg.

Examiner Hand Position

Hands cupped behind the tibia and fibula away from the site of pain.

Evaluation Procedure

Gentlly squeeze (compress) the tibia and fibula, adding more pressure if no symptoms are elicitied.

Positive Test

Pain is elicited, especially when away from the injured area.

Implications

Gross fracture or stress fracture of the tibia and/or fibula, syndesmostic sprain.

Modifications/Comments

Work towards site of injury; avoid applying too much pressure too soon; pressure should be applied progressively and gradually; a normal compression test may be done by cupping the dorsum of the foot.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 30%
  • Specificity: 94%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
