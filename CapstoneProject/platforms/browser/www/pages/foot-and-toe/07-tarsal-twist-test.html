Foot & Toe

Tarsal Twist Test (Dorsiflexion-Eversion Test)

Patient Position

Sitting with legs off the table.

Examiner Position

At the patient's feet.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand cupping the calcaneous. Opposite hand placed on distal plantar aspect of the foot (near distal metatarsals and toes).

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Passively evert the calcaneous and talus, dorsiflex the foot and toes.
  2. Hold this position for 5-10 seconds.

Positive Test

Pain and/or parathesisa radiating into the foot.

Implications

Posterior tibial nerve dysfunction.

Modifications/Comments

Tinel Sign may be done over the course of this procedure.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 81%
  • Specificity: 99%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
