Shoulder

O'Brien Test (Active Compression Test)

Patient Position

Standing with GH joint flexed to 90 degrees and horizontally adducted 15 degrees from the sagittal plane.

Examiner Position

In front of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand placed over the superior aspect of the patient's distal forearm.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient resists the examiner's downward force.
  2. The test is repeated with the humerus externally rotated and the forearm supinated.

Positive Test

Pain or clicking.

Implications

Pain or clicking within the GH joint may indicate a labral tear. Pain at the AC joint may indicate AC pathology.
