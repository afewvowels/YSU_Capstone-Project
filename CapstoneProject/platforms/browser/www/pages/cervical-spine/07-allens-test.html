Cervical Spine

Allen's Test

Patient Position

Seated.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the patient's affected shoulder, and the distal hand's index and middle finger palpating the patient's radial pulse.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passive abducts the patient's affected shoulder to 90 degrees, elbow passively flexed to 90 degrees, passively externally rotates and horizontally abducts arm.
  2. The patient then actively rotates away from the affected side.

Positive Test

Radial pulse disappears.

Implications

Pectoralis minor is compressing the neurovascular bundle.

Modifications/Comments

Test may present a false-positive.

Evidence

N/A
