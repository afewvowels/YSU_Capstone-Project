Knee

Slocum Test

Patient Position

Lying supine with knee flexed to 90 degrees.

Examiner Position

sitting on patient's foot (A) foot is internally rotated 25 degrees to test for anterolateral capsule (B) foot is externally rotated 15 degrees to test the anteromedial capsule.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand grasps the tibial joint line right behind the knee with thumbs placed along the joint line on both sides of the patellar tendon, while index fingers are along hamstring tendons to ensure they are relaxed.

Evaluation Procedure

  • Tibia is drawn anteriorly.

    • Positive Test

    Increased amount of tibial anterior translation or lack of a firm endpoint.

    Implications

    1. anterolateral instability, damage to the ACL, anterolateral capsule, LCL, IT band strain, Popliteus tendon strain, Posterolateral complex, lateral meniscus.
    2. Anteromedial instability, damage to MCL, anteromedial capsule sprain, ACL sprain, posteriomedial capsule, pes ansurine strain, medial meniscus.

    Modifications/Comments

    excessive rotation may cause false negative due to wedging of the mensici in the joint space.
