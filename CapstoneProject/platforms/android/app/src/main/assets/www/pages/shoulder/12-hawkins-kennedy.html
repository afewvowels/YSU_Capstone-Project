Shoulder

Hawkins-Kennedy Test

Back

Patient Position

Sitting or standing with shoulder, elbow, and wrist in anatomical position.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral or in front of the involved side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand grasping the forearm at the elbow joint and the other hand on the patient's wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. With the elbow flexed, the GH joint is elevated to 90 degrees in the scapular plane.
  2. The humerus is then internally rotated until pain is experienced.

Positive Test

Pain with ROM, especially towards full ROM.

Implications

Impingement of the rotator cuff muscles (especially the supraspinatus) or the long head of the biceps brachii. Test may also present as a SLAP lesion.

Modifications/Comments

Must be performed in scapular plane or false positive will be elicited.

Evidence

Impingement:

  • Sensitivity: 75%
  • Specificity: 52%

SLAP Lesion:

  • Sensitivity: 61%
  • Specificity: 39%
