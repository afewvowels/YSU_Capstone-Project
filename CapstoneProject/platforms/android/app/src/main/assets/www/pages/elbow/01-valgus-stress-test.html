Elbow

Valgus Stress Test

Back

Patient Position

Seated or standing.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient's affected elbow.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the lateral epicondyle and the distal hand grasping the patient's distal forearm.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively flexes the patient's elbow to 25 degrees.
  2. The examiner then applies a valgus force to the elbow.
  3. The procedure is then done in multiple angles with the elbow flexed.

Positive Test

Increased laxity, pain, or both when compared bilaterally.

Implications

Ulnar Collateral Ligament Sprain (anterior oblique position).

Modifications/Comments

Laxity beyond 60 degrees of flexion indiciates a Ulnar Collateral Ligament Sprain in the posterior oblique position. Laxity may also indicate a epiphyseal injury
