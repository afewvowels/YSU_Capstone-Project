Shoulder

Jerk Test

Back

Patient Position

Supine or seated (supine is advised).

Examiner Position

Behind the patient or on the affected side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand stabilizes the scapula. The opposite hand holds the affected arm at 90 degrees of flexion and internal rotation.

Evaluation Procedure

The affected arm is passively horizontally adducted while the examiner maintains axial load to the humerus.

Positive Test

"Clunk" in the shoulder or pain.

Implications

Posteroinferior instability with or without posteroinferior labral tear.

Modifications/Comments

A painful clunk is associated with a labral tear that indicates the need for surgery.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 52%
  • Specificity: 94%
