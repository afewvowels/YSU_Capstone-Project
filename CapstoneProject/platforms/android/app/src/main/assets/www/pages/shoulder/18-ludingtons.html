Ludington's Test

Back

Patient Position

Standing or sitting with hands on top of the head with fingers interlocked.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient on the affected side.

Examiner Hand Position

Palpating each long head of the biceps tendon.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Patient contracts the biceps brachii by applying force to the top of the head.
  2. During this motion, the examiner palpates the long head of the biceps tendon.

Positive Test

Decreased or no tension is felt in the involved tendon. Pain is increased.

Implications

Rupture of the long head of the biceps tendon.
