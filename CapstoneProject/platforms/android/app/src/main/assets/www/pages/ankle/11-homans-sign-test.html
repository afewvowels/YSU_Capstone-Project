Ankle

Homans' Sign Test

Patient Position

Lying supine with patient's involved foot of the table

Examiner Position

At the foot of the patient on the lateral side

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand cupping the posterior calf and distal hand on the plantar surface

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively dorsiflexes the foot in full knee extension.
  2. Examiner then applies pressure on the gastrocnemius.

Positive Test

Reproduction of pain in the calf

Implications

Deep vein thrombosis

Modifications/Comments

If a postiive test, this is a medial emergency. Pain may be elicited on palpation

Evidence

Inconclusive
