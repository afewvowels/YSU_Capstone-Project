Shoulder

Surprise Test (Anterior Release Test)

Back

Patient Position

Laying supine on table with shoulder abducted to 90 degrees and elbow flexed to 90 degrees.

Examiner Position

Beside patient on the involved side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand on anterior shoulder and other grasping the forearm proximal to the wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

Examiner removes the hand that was applying the posterior pressure.

Positive Test

Apprehension or pain when the anterior stabilizing pressure from the relocation test is removed.

Implications

Reproduces the apprehension test.

Modifications/Comments

Clinical use is not recommended.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 92%
  • Specificity: 89%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
