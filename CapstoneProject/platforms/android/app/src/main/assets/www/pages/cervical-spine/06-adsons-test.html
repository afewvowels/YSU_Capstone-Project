Cervical Spine

Adson's Test

Patient Position

Seated.

Examiner Position

Standing behind the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the patient's affected shoulder, and the distal hand's index and middle finger palpating the patient's radial pulse.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passive abducts the patient's affected shoulder to 30 degrees, elbow actively extended, and passively externally rotates the humerus.
  2. The patient then actively extends and rotates toward the affected side.
  3. Patient then inhales deeply and holds their breath.

Positive Test

The radial pulse diminishes or disappears.

Implications

Subclavian artery is occluded between the anterior and middle scalene muscles, and the pectoralis minor.

Modifications/Comments

Test may present a false-positive.

Evidence

N/A
