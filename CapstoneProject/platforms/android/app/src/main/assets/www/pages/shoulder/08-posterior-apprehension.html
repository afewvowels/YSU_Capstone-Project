Shoulder

Posterior Apprehension Test

Patient Position

Supine on table. Shoulder flexed to 90 degrees and adducted 90 degrees. Elbow flexed to 90 degrees. Shoulder should be off of the side of table.

Examiner Position

Standing on the affected side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand grasps the forearm at the elbow joint. The other hand stabilizes the posterior scapula.

Evaluation Procedure

Examiner applies a posterior force to the humeral shaft, forcing the humeral head to move posteriorly.

Positive Test

Patient displays apprehension and produces muscle guarding to prevent the shoulder from subluxing posteriorly.

Implications

Laxity in the posterior glenohumeral capsule, torn posterior labrum.

Modifications/Comments

Joint angles can be altered in order to test various portions of the shoulder.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 19%
  • Specificity: 99%

Evidence Resource

Starkey C, Brown SD. Examination of Orthopedic & Athletic Injuries. 4th ed. Philadelphia: F.A. Davis Company; 2015.
