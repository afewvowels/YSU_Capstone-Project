Cervical Spine

Vertebral Artery Test

Back

Patient Position

Lying supine on table.

Examiner Position

sitting at the head of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Supporting the subject's head.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. the examinar slowly extends, rotates, and laterally flexes the subject's cervical spine to each side.
  2. Each position should be held for about 30 seconds.

Positive Test

The examiner observes the subject for dizziness, blurred vision, nystagmus, slurred speech, or loss of conciousness.

Implications

Partial or complete occulsion of the vertebral artery.

Modifications/Comments

The signs and symptoms experienced in this test are considered contraindications for treatments such as traction and joint mobilizations.
