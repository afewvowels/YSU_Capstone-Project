Tinel's Sign

Back

Patient Position

Seated or standing.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Distal hand is wrapped around the distal forearm and the proximal hand will be used for the examination.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Examiner passively flexes the patient's elbow to about 30 degrees.
  2. The examiner will use the index finger and the middle finger of the proximal hand to tap along the ulnar groove.

Positive Test

Patient experiences radiating pain down the forearm and possibly into the hand.

Implications

Ulnar nerve impingement or trauma.
