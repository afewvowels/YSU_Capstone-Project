Shoulder

Speed's Test

Back

Patient Position

Sitting or standing with elbow extended and GH joint in neutral position or slightly extended.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral and in front of the involved side.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand palpates the bicipital groove with the other hand stabilizing at the wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. Clinician resists flexion of the GH joint while palpating for tenderness over the bicipital groove.
  2. Allow patient to move through ROM.

Positive Test

Pain along the long head of biceps tendon or superior shoulder.

Implications

Inflammation of the LHBT, tear of the transverse humeral ligament, SLAP lesion.

Modifications/Comments

N/A

Evidence

Bicipital Involvement:

  • Sensitivity: 56%
  • Specificity: 57%

SLAP Lesion:

  • Sensitivity: 44%
  • Specificity: 64%
