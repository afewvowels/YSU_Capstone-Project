Yergason's Test

Back

Patient Position

Sitting or standing with elbow flexed to 90 degrees and forearm pronated.

Examiner Position

Lateral to the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

One hand palpates the bicipital groove, the other hand stabilizes the forearm proximal to the wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

Patient provides resistance while the examiner moves the GH joint into external rotation while resisting forearm supination and elbow flexion.

Positive Test

Pain or snapping at the bicipital groove or pain at the superior GH joint (SLAP lesion).

Implications

Tear or laxity of the transverse humeral ligament. Bicipital tendonitis, SLAP lesion.

Modifications/Comments

Pain with no associated popping in the bicipital groove indicated tendinopoty.

Evidence

Bicipital Involvement:

  • Sensitivity: 39%
  • Specificity: 83%

SLAP Lesion:

  • Sensitivity: 13%
  • Specificity: 93%
