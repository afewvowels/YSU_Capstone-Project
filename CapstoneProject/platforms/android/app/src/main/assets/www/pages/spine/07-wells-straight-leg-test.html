Thoracic & Lumbar Spine

Wells Straight Leg Test

Patient Position

supine.

Examiner Position

at the side of the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

On the patient's lower leg of the uninvolved side.

Evaluation Procedure

The patient lies supine and the examinar passively raises the patient's leg by flexing their hip to 90 degrees or until pain is elicited while keeping the knee extended.

Positive Test

Pain, numbness, or tingling radiating in low back, glut, or down the leg.

Implications

lumbar/sacral pathology and/or disc herniation.
