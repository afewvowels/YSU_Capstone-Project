Knee

Apley Compression/Distraction Test

Patient Position

Lying prone with knee flexed to 90 degrees.

Examiner Position

Standing next to involved side.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand on the distal thigh for stabilization and distal hand on the subject's heel.

Evaluation Procedure

With the distal hand, the examiner applies a downward force while internally and externally rotating heel

Positive Test

Pain, clicking, or restriction.

Implications

Mensicus tear.

Modifications/Comments

Test may be repeated with a distraction force to alleviate pain. Increase or change in location of pain may be indicitive of ligamentous structure.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 16%
  • Specificity: 90%

Evidence Resource

Konin, Jeff G., et al. Special Tests for Orthopedic Examination. 4th ed. SLACK Incorporated; 2016.
