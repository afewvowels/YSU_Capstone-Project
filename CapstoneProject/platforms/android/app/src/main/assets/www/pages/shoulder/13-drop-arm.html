Shoulder

Drop Arm Test

Back

Patient Position

Standing or sitting. Humerus fully abducted.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to or behind involved extremity.

Examiner Hand Position

N/A (see modification).

Evaluation Procedure

The patient slowly lowers the arm to the side.

Positive Test

Arm falls uncontrollably from a position of approximately 90 degrees abduction to the side. Pain may be replicated.

Implications

Rotator cuff lesions, especially supraspinatus.

Modifications/Comments

If arm is controlled through ROM, place the arm at 90 degrees abduction. Then, the examiner places a gentle downward pressure at the distal forearm. Positive test would be the arm falling/not able to hold the position.

Evidence

  • Sensitivity: 46%
  • Specificity: 74%

Evidence Resource

Starkey C, Brown SD. Examination of Orthopedic & Athletic Injuries. 4th ed. Philadelphia: F.A. Davis Company; 2015.
