Elbow

Test for Medial Epicondylitis

Back

Patient Position

Seated.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand's thumb is placed just inferior to the medial epicondyle and the distal hand grasps the patient's wrist.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. The examiner passively flexes the elbow to 90 degrees, and the patient's fingers are actively flexed.
  2. The examiner passively supinates the forearm and extends the elbow.

Positive Test

Pain over the medial epicondyle or at the common attachment site of the wrist flexor group.

Implications

Medial epicondylitis (Golfer's Elbow).
