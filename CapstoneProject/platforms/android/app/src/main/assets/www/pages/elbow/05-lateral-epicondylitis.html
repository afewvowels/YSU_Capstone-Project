Elbow

Test for Lateral Epicondylitis

Back

Patient Position

Seated.

Examiner Position

Standing lateral to the patient.

Examiner Hand Position

Proximal hand's thumb is placed just inferior to the lateral epicondyle and the distal hand is placed on the dorsal side of the patient's affected hand.

Evaluation Procedure

  1. The examiner passively flexes the elbow to 90 degrees, the forearm is passively prontated, and the patient's fingers are actively flexed.
  2. The patient then resists the force of the examiner, while the examiner tries to force the wrist into flexion.

Positive Test

Pain over the lateral epicondyle or at the common attachment site of the wrist extensor group.

Implications

Lateral epicondylitis (Tennis Elbow).
